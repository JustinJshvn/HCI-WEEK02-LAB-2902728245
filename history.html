History of Yogyakarta

Home | History | Attractions

The area around Yogyakarta has deep roots in the Hindu-Buddhist Mataram era and later became the seat of the Yogyakarta Sultanate. After Indonesian independence, Yogyakarta briefly served as the national capital (1946-1948), reflecting its important role in the country’s early years.

Kraton (The Sultan's Palace)

The Kraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat is the royal palace and the cultural heart of the city. It remains an active residence and a living museum of Javanese traditions.
